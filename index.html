Meta Ads
Facebook & Instagram. Where ~60–70% of your digital budget goes and where most pseudo campaigns start. The platform has multiple ad fields — each with its own limit and its own audience exposure.

Why Marketing Must Always Provide 5+ Headline Options
💡
Meta's automatic A/B testing: When Digital sets up an ad set, they can enter multiple headlines and Meta's algorithm automatically rotates them — showing different versions to different users and identifying which drives the most leads. If Marketing provides only one headline, Digital loses this optimization advantage and CPL (cost per lead) goes up. Always give Digital 5 headlines to choose from.

Hashtag Rules — Facebook vs. Instagram
Facebook
  • !Avoid hashtags in Facebook ad copy. They do not improve reach in ads and can look spammy. Facebook's algorithm in ads does not use hashtags for discovery the way organic posts do.
  • ✓If you must use hashtags in organic Facebook posts (not ads), use 1–3 maximum.
  • ✓Use CTA text instead of hashtags — "Register now," "Book a site visit," "WhatsApp us" convert far better.
Instagram
  • ✓For Instagram ads: use 3–5 highly relevant hashtags. More does not mean more reach in ads.
  • ✓For Instagram organic posts (reels, carousels): 5–10 targeted hashtags. Place at the end of caption, not mixed into text.
  • !Do not use generic hashtags like #realestate or #mumbai — these drown in volume. Use specific ones: #ThaneWest2BHK #MumbaiHomes #XanaduRealty
  • !Never use banned/irrelevant hashtags. Instagram suppresses reach when these are detected.
What to Write — Phase by Phase
Pseudo Phase
Before pricing is revealed. Build curiosity.
  • Use location & lifestyle cues
  • Indicative price: "Starting ~₹85L"
  • CTA: "Register your interest"
  • No floor plans, no exact sizes
  • Intrigue > Information
Teaser Phase
Post CP Meet. Countdown energy.
  • Build urgency: "Launching soon"
  • Drive pre-registrations
  • CTA: "Pre-register for priority pricing"
  • Tease the campaign theme/visual
  • Countdown if date confirmed
Reveal + Mop Up
Full launch. Drive bookings.
  • Exact price, config, carpet area
  • Pay plan as USP
  • CTA: "Book now / Site visit"
  • Mop up: "Limited units left"
  • Social proof if available
Google Ads
Search captures high-intent buyers actively looking. Display re-targets those who've visited your landing pages. These users are already in the market — copy must be direct and keyword-rich.
Search · Display · YouTube
ℹ️
Responsive Search Ads (RSA) — the key format: Google lets you supply up to 15 headlines and 4 descriptions. Google's AI then tests different combinations automatically and shows the best-performing mix to each user. Marketing should aim for 10+ headlines and 3–4 descriptions so Google has enough combinations to optimise.

✓ Do this
Write in first person: "I thought you'd want to know about this before it goes live publicly."
Use the lead's name in the first line if CRM allows: "Hi Rahul,"
One clear CTA per message. "Reply YES to get the brochure" or "Click below for prices."
Keep paragraphs to 1–2 lines. Short = readable on mobile.
Use *bold* for the single most important detail: price, dates, offer.
✗ Avoid this
Never start with "Dear Sir/Ma'am." Feels like spam immediately.
Don't use hashtags on WhatsApp — they serve no function and look strange.
Don't send walls of text. If you need more than 5 lines, restructure.
Don't put more than 2 CTAs: "Call / WhatsApp / Visit / Register / Download" = user confusion = no action.
Don't make the first line promotional: "GREAT OFFER! LIMITED TIME!" — leads block this immediately.

SMS Templates
SMS has the highest open rate of any channel (98%) but the least patience. Every character costs money — literally. TRAI regulations also govern what you can and cannot send.
SMS · Bulk · DLT
160
Characters in a single SMS. Beyond this, it splits into 2 messages (153 chars each) and costs double.
98%
Open rate for SMS. Higher than email (20%), higher than WhatsApp blasts. But also lower trust.
7s
Average time a user decides whether to engage or dismiss an SMS. Hook must be instant.
TRAI / DLT Rules — Critical for Compliance
⚠️
All SMS templates must be pre-registered on the DLT (Distributed Ledger Technology) platform before they can be sent. This is an Indian regulatory requirement. Marketing must finalize the exact template wording and share it with Digital/XCC who register it. You cannot deviate from the registered template — even one word change requires re-registration.
  • !Template must include brand name / sender ID
  • !Must not promise returns or guaranteed income (SEBI issue)
  • !Opt-out option required for promotional SMS
  • ✓Transactional SMS (booking confirmation, OTP) has different rules — check with Digital
  • !No special characters like &, <, > — can break SMS delivery
  • !Avoid smart quotes (" ") — use straight quotes (" ") or omit
  • ✓Include project RERA number if advertising pricing
  • ✓Short link mandatory — use bit.ly or similar (Digital manages)

Website & Landing Page
Every rupee of digital spend drives traffic to a landing page. If the page doesn't convert, the budget is wasted. These are the basics Marketing should understand when briefing the agency.
Landing Page · Microsite · Project Website
💡
The Golden Rule: A landing page has one job — convert a visitor into a lead. Every element should serve that job or be removed. Marketing should never request elements that look good but don't convert (e.g. animated intros that delay content, pop-up videos, long brand narratives above the fold).
The Above-the-Fold Rule

Everything a visitor sees before scrolling is "above the fold." On mobile (where 80%+ of your traffic lands), this is roughly 600px — about 4 lines of text plus a button. If the price, project name, and form/CTA are not visible immediately, you lose the visitor.

  • ✓Project name + location must be visible immediately
  • ✓Starting price — the single highest converting piece of information
  • ✓One CTA button — "Book a Site Visit" or "Get Price Details"
  • ✓A high-quality elevation image or render as the hero visual
  • !No autoplay video above the fold — kills load speed
  • !No animated intros or splash screens — visitors bounce before they load

Lead Form — Keep It Short
ℹ️
Every extra field in a form reduces conversion rate by 10–15%. A form asking for Name, Phone, Email, Budget, Configuration, and Possession Timeline will convert far fewer visitors than a form asking for Name and Phone only. Start minimal; XCC qualifies the rest on the call.
Ideal Form — 2 Fields
  • ✓Name
  • ✓Phone Number
Highest conversion. XCC qualifies rest on the call.
Acceptable — 3 Fields
  • ✓Name
  • ✓Phone Number
  • ✓Email (optional)
Adds email for drip campaigns. Still converts well.
Avoid — 4+ Fields
  • !Name + Phone + Email + Budget + Config + Possession
Converts poorly. Use only for premium / ultra-HNI products where lead quality > volume.

CTA Button Copy — What Works vs. What Doesn't
✓ High-converting CTAs
"Get Price Details" — leads to an immediate, specific action
"Book a Free Site Visit" — free reduces friction
"Download Brochure" — clear value exchange
"Check Unit Availability" — creates urgency via scarcity framing
"Get Today's Offer" — time-sensitive, benefit-led
✗ Low-converting CTAs
"Submit" — tells the user nothing about what happens next
"Learn More" — vague, non-committal
"Click Here" — no value, no context
"Contact Us" — sounds like a customer complaint portal
"Enquire Now" — passive. Leads, not customers, enquire.
Real Estate Copy Principles
Platform rules are the constraint. These principles are the craft. They apply across every channel — Meta, Google, WhatsApp, SMS, website, and the CP Kit messaging guide.
Universal · All Channels
Principle 1
Lead with the buyer's pain, not the product's features
MMR buyers feel: rent drain, long commute, cramped space, status anxiety. Your first line should name their problem, not describe your amenities.
Principle 2
Price is always the most powerful piece of copy
If you know the price, put it in the first line. "Starting ₹85L" outperforms any lifestyle line in click-through rate, consistently, every time. Don't bury it.
Principle 3
One message, one action per ad
Don't ask users to "Call / WhatsApp / Visit / Register / Download." Pick one. A confused user does nothing. Every ad should have one CTA and one desired outcome.
Principle 4
Commute is the #1 location trigger for MMR
Don't say "central location." Say "12 minutes to Thane Station" or "35 min to BKC." Specific, provable commute time converts better than vague location descriptors.
Principle 5
Urgency must be real, not manufactured
"Limited units" works — once. If every launch has "only 5 units left" for 3 months, buyers stop believing it. Use real urgency signals: price revision date, inventory data, genuine deadlines.
Principle 6
The pay plan is a pricing tool, not a footnote
"10:90 plan" or "No EMI till possession" changes the buyer's mental maths entirely. A ₹1.2Cr home feels attainable when the CTA is "Book at ₹12L." Always surface the pay plan.
